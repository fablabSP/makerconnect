The Network

Two Tiers. One Journey.

Schools typically enter as Learning Nodes and progress to Learning Nexus as their maker education capability deepens. Both tiers are part of the same connected network.

Entry Tier · Exploratory · Annual
Learning Node
Exploring or piloting maker education for the first time.
SP FabLab Provides
  • Invitations to Learning Journeys, sharing sessions and community events
  • Responsive, chargeable support aligned to school interests and readiness
  • Access to MakerConnect community of practice
Annual Expectations
  • Participate in 1 SP FabLab Learning Journey or Onboarding Programme
  • Extend an invitation to one fellow educator from another school
  • Attend or be represented at MakerConnect annually
Commitment: Low — Exploratory, participation-based. Annual, renewable.
Strategic Tier · Developmental · Multi-year
Learning Nexus
Embedding maker education school-wide at curriculum and infrastructure level.
SP FabLab Provides
  • 3-tier structured student Maker Education programme (~60 hours)
  • CET-accredited or customised teacher professional development
  • Makerspace planning consultancy and SP FabLab Space Design Kit
  • Priority access to SP FabLab facilities, expertise, and joint pilots
  • Formal recognition as SP FabLab Maker Education Learning Nexus
Annual Expectations
  • Conduct at least 1 teacher-led Maker Education workshop
  • Deliver 1 cluster or professional community sharing
  • Engage students in 3-tier programme (~60 hours)
  • Minimum 5–9 trained staff via SP CET programmes annually
  • Makerspace readiness — existing or demonstrated intent
Commitment: High — School leadership-supported, teacher-led. Multi-year, reviewed annually.
